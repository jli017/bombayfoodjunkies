E — Ethics

Ethical Sourcing

Commitment to transparency in the supply chain and fair labor practices. Here's our research on the top ethical brands and what it truly costs to source responsibly.

🌿 How to Source Ethically

  • Look for Fair Trade Certified, Rainforest Alliance, or B Corp labels on products.
  • Vet all food and material suppliers for fair labor certifications before onboarding.
  • Prioritize local, minority-owned, and transparent businesses that share SEED values.
  • Request a Supplier Code of Conduct agreement from all vendors.
  • Conduct annual audits of your top 5 suppliers.
  • Utilize platforms like Good Food Purchasing Program or Sustainable Restaurant Association for verified vendor lists.

📊 The Cost of Ethical Sourcing

Ethical sourcing typically adds 10–35% more to ingredient and supply costs. However, this is offset by:

  • Increased customer loyalty from conscious consumers
  • Brand differentiation in a competitive market
  • Reduced regulatory and reputational risk
  • Alignment with growing Gen Z and Millennial spending values

Studies show 66% of consumers will pay more for sustainable brands (Nielsen, 2023).

Implementation

Program Timeline

A 24-month phased rollout designed to build sustainable systems without disrupting daily operations.

PHASE 1: PLATFORM & RESEARCH
(Months 1–4)
Objectives:
  • Launch social media advertising to build awareness.
  • Conduct sourcing research on sustainable suppliers.
  • Host initial community events.
  • Begin drafting the Employee Treatment Manual.
PHASE 2: SYSTEM DEVELOPMENT
(Months 5–9)
Objectives:
  • Finalize and implement the Employee Treatment Manual.
  • Introduce ethical sourcing into operations.
  • Develop internal sustainability checklist.
  • Continue expanding community engagement.
PHASE 3: IMPLEMENTATION
(Months 10–18)
Objectives:
  • Roll out composting routes and waste systems.
  • Host community cooking demonstrations.
  • Launch sustainable community events.
  • Begin collecting customer feedback.
PHASE 4: FEEDBACK & EXPANSION
(Months 19–24)
Objectives:
  • Conduct employee treatment policy evaluations.
  • Gather and analyze restaurant feedback.
  • Implement improvements based on data.
  • Expand sustainability initiatives long-term.
